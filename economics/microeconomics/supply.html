Supply

Supply is defined as the quantity of goods that a producer is willing to sell at a particular price during a particular period of time, assuming that other factors are constant.

Supply is provided not only by the manufacturer of the goods, but also by the owners of the resources (land, labor, etc.). From the resource supplier's point of view, supply can be defined as the amount of resources that a resource owner can and willingly supply during a period at a particular price, provided that other factors are constant.

There must be both the willingness and the ability to make the supply present to constitute supply. Therefore, if a producer intends to sell 6,000 units of product daily, but has a capacity of only 2,000 units per day, the supply will be 2,000 units instead of 6,000 units.

Economists usually present supply information as a series of supply levels at different prices in a table called a supply schedule. The plotted supply schedule produces a curvilinear graph called the supply curve.
