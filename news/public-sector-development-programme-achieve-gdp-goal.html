PSDP set at Rs.900bn to achieve 4.8pc GDP

ISLAMABAD: The Annual Plan Coordination Committee (APCC) on Friday finalised Rs.900 billion worth of Public Sector Development Programme (PSDP) to achieve an economic growth rate of 4.8 per cent in the next fiscal year (2021-2022).

The PSDP for next year is over 38pc higher than the current year's allocation of Rs.650bn, while GDP growth target of 4.8pc is conservatively higher than the current year's provisional output estimate of 3.94pc.

The APCC meeting was presided over planning Commission Deputy Chairman Jahanzeb Khan. Speaking at a press conference, Minister for Planning and Development Asad Umar said the conservative estimate for growth was based on understaning that large-scale manufacturing and agriculture had bounced back this year after a decline of two years and would moderate next year.

He said cotton was the only crop which had been going down in recent years, declining by over two million bales to 7 million this year and hece a focused attempt would begin at the start of the season with good seed and guality pesticides to recoup its production well above 10.5 million bales, including 4 million bales in Sindh and 6 million bales in Punjab and the rest in other provinces. He said the rising international prices would also help lift cotton production in the country.

The minister said the provinces were expected to put together about Rs.1 trillion for their annual development programmes for next year. He said the Public Private Partnership Authority of the federal government was expencted to start almost Rs.1 trillion worth of development programes next year through private sector investments through Rs.70 billion funds it would be getting from the PSDP.

The Rs.900 billion PSDP involves foreign aid of Rs.101 billion. The ministries and divisions, provincial governments and Azad Jammu and Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan had forwarded total demands worth Rs.1.5 trillion for next year and about Rs.600 billion worth of projects had to be dropped.

Govt of Pakistan is looking all these prospects for uplifting the gross domenstic product of Pakistan to 4.8 per cent in the comming fiscal year.
